The retrieval pipeline is now returning relevant context, but there is still a critical issue with the Section Mapper Agent.

## Current Problem

The retrieved context is generally correct, but the Section Mapper sometimes outputs incorrect BNS/BNSS/BSA sections even though the correct legal text exists in the retrieved documents.

It appears the LLM is guessing or reasoning about the section number instead of extracting it from the retrieved context.

For a legal RAG system, this behavior is unacceptable. Every cited section must be grounded in the retrieved documents.

## Required Fix

### 1. Never Hallucinate Section Numbers

The Section Mapper must never generate section numbers from model knowledge.

It should only identify sections that explicitly appear in the retrieved documents.

If a section number is not present in the retrieved context, the agent must return:

* "No applicable section found in retrieved documents."

instead of inventing one.

---

### 2. Search Across All Four Legal Documents

The retrieval pipeline must search all indexed legal documents for every query:

* Constitution of India
* Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)
* Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS)
* Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA)

Do not restrict retrieval to a single document.

Retrieve relevant chunks from every applicable source.

The retriever should merge and rerank results from all four documents before passing them to downstream agents.

---

### 3. Improve Hybrid Retrieval

The retrieval process should:

* Perform dense vector search.
* Perform BM25 keyword search.
* Retrieve results from all four legal documents.
* Merge the candidate results.
* Apply BGE Reranker.
* Pass only the highest-ranked chunks to the agents.

The reranker should determine which legal provisions are most relevant regardless of which document they come from.

---

### 4. Section Extraction Instead of Prediction

The Section Mapper should work like an information extraction component.

Its job is to extract:

* BNS Sections
* BNSS Sections
* BSA Sections
* Constitutional Articles

from the retrieved chunks.

It should not infer missing section numbers.

If multiple sections are relevant, return all of them.

---

### 5. Include Source Metadata

Every retrieved chunk should carry metadata such as:

* Document Name
* Chapter
* Section Number
* Article Number (if Constitution)
* Chunk ID

The Section Mapper should rely on this metadata instead of asking the LLM to guess section numbers.

If the metadata already contains the section number, use that directly.

---

### 6. Validator Responsibilities

The Validator Agent must verify that:

* Every cited section exists in the retrieved chunk.
* The document name matches the section.
* The quoted text belongs to that section.
* No fabricated citations are included.

If validation fails, remove the incorrect citation instead of returning it.

---

### 7. Quote Selector

The Quote Selector should extract the exact statutory language from the retrieved section.

Every quote should include:

* Document Name
* Section/Article Number
* Exact excerpt from the retrieved chunk

Never paraphrase statutory text when quoting.

---

### 8. Final Response Format

The final response should clearly separate information by source.

Example:

### Applicable Legal Provisions

**BNS**

* Section XXX
* Summary
* Exact quotation

**BNSS**

* Section XXX
* Summary
* Exact quotation

**BSA**

* Section XXX
* Summary
* Exact quotation

**Constitution of India**

* Article XX
* Summary
* Exact quotation

This ensures the system retrieves and cites from all four legal documents whenever applicable.

---

### 9. Improve Retrieval Accuracy

Increase retrieval quality by:

* Retrieving Top-30 chunks initially.
* Applying BGE reranking.
* Keeping the Top-8 to Top-10 chunks.
* Deduplicating overlapping chunks.
* Preserving metadata throughout the pipeline.

---

### 10. Strict Grounding Policy

The legal assistant must operate in a retrieval-first manner.

Every legal statement, section number, article number, quotation, and citation must come directly from the retrieved documents.

If the information cannot be found in the retrieved context, the assistant must explicitly state that no supporting legal provision was retrieved rather than generating one from model memory.

Please refactor the orchestration so that section mapping is based on document extraction and metadata, not LLM reasoning. The final system should behave like a production-grade legal search engine where citations are always verifiable and grounded in the indexed legal corpus.
